The Story

One Problem.
No Exit.

A 22-year-old college dropout — who technically has the knowledge of a business school graduate but never filed the final paperwork — becomes the most dangerous player no one sees coming. Not because he wants power. Because he needs to solve one finite math problem: save his family and disappear.

Set against Seattle's dual identity — Amazon money and displaced Black neighborhoods, legal weed and illegal moves, tech surveillance and street survival — Rain City is a psychological crime drama where the protagonist's greatest weapon is being completely underestimated.

But the game doesn't negotiate. Every time Ace gets close to his number, something happens. The goal keeps moving. The "one last job" becomes ten. The exit keeps getting further away.

Where It Lives

Seattle / Tacoma
The Perfect Storm

🏙️

Tech Boom

Amazon and Microsoft money creating two cities in one — the hyper-wealthy and the systematically displaced. Gentrification is legal ethnic cleansing with a LEED certification and a rooftop terrace.

🌿

Legal Weed

Washington's legalization created new game, new players, old rules that don't apply. The grey market between legal and illegal becomes Ace's first foothold — and first trap.

🌧️

The Rain

Not dramatic thunder and lightning. Persistent. Inevitable. Wearing away at everything over time. A visual heartbeat. A metaphor. Evidence washed away but choices remain.

🏚️

Street Politics

Gentrification destroying Black neighborhoods, turf wars during displacement, old generation vs. new methods. South Seattle, Rainier Valley, the Central District — communities being slowly erased.

The Hook

What Makes This
Different

01

The Goal Is To Leave, Not To Win

Traditional crime dramas: protagonist wants power, empire, respect. Rain City: Ace wants to solve a $94,770 problem and disappear. The tragedy is structural — every time he gets close, something happens. A new family emergency. A new person needing help. A new threat forcing him deeper. The exit keeps getting further away. Can you ever really leave?

02

Psychological Thriller Elements

Ace has panic attacks no one knows about. Insomnia from constant calculation. Code-switching trauma — exhausted from maintaining different versions of himself for every person in his life. The horror: watching a good man slowly become someone he doesn't recognize. Mirrors, rain, numbers, silence — all visual representations of his fracturing identity.

03

Tech Thriller in the Digital Age

Ace's methods: encrypted apps, cryptocurrency, gig economy exploitation (DoorDash drivers unknowingly moving product), corporate logistics knowledge, social media OSINT. Chen's methods: phone record analysis, financial transaction tracking, surveillance tech. The cat-and-mouse is digital. Who's smarter with technology?

04

Hyper-Realistic Money Math

Actual numbers. Actual math. WHERE money goes, not just "getting rich." The grind isn't glamorous. Violence is ugly and traumatic, not cool. Addiction destroys communities — shown honestly. The system is the real villain. No easy answers. Good people do bad things. Bad people have valid points.

05

The Good Man Doing Bad Things

Ace is empathetic. He feels guilty constantly. He throws up after his first violent confrontation. He has nightmares. He cries alone in his car at 3 AM. He goes to church with Grandma and feels like a hypocrite. Good intentions don't matter. The game doesn't care why you're playing.

The Arc

Multi-Season
Structure

S1
Phase 1: The Organic Start

Season One — Learning the Game

Weed, pills, small-time hustling. Slow build ($12–15K total over the season). Ace learns the infrastructure, the players, the risks. Marcus begins mentioning his connect. Season ends with Ace realizing small-time isn't enough — the decision point is approaching.

S2
Phase 2: Marcus's Connect

Season Two — Real Product, Real Stakes

Working with Junior — a mid-level dealer. Real product (coke, molly). Better money ($8–15K per month). Troy appears. Elena gets mentioned. Junior's limitations become obvious. Season ends: Ace at crossroads — level up or stay stuck.

S3
Phase 3: Someone From the Past

Season Three — The Real Players

Troy connects Ace to Elena. Better supply. Real dealer territory. Serious money ($20–30K per month). Elena tests Ace for a bigger role. Mateo is introduced. Season ends: Ace passes Elena's test. He's about to enter the real game.

S4
Phase 4: The Real Game

Season Four+ — Too Deep To Leave

Ace as a real player. Cartel connections. Big money — bigger problems. Everything he built starts collapsing. Consequences catch up. The exit is no longer a door. The question becomes: does he die, go to prison, or actually escape?
