RAIN CITY // Season One Novel

"The goal is to leave, not to win."

A 22-year-old college dropout — who carries the knowledge of a business school graduate without the diploma — becomes the most dangerous player no one sees coming. Not because he wants power. Because he needs to solve one finite math problem: save his family and disappear. But in a world where everyone wants a piece of him, leaving might be the one thing he can't do.

Chapter One — The Heartbeat

Chapter One — The Heartbeat

The heartbeat came first.

Not the metaphorical kind. Not the poetic kind that people meant when they said follow your heart. A physical thump, low and stubborn, running under everything like a second bassline the world couldn't mute.

Ace felt it in his throat before he heard it anywhere else. A pulse pushing against his windpipe every time he swallowed. A slow, deliberate percussion that made his body feel like a machine running without permission.

THUMP.

He stared at the security monitors and tried to pretend it was just the hum of the lights, the rain on the windows, the ancient HVAC cycling like it was clearing its lungs.

THUMP.

The warehouse camera feeds rotated through their loop: aisle three, aisle four, loading dock, employee lot. Gray. Grainy. Empty.

His eyes kept doing what they always did on third shift: scanning for motion, scanning for shapes, scanning for the one pixel that might change and force him to move. He could do it in his sleep.

Tonight his posture was perfect.

That was the problem.

Perfect posture meant tension. Tension meant the body knew something the mind was refusing to name.

THUMP.

His phone sat hidden in his lap below the desk line, brightness turned down until the screen was more bruise than light. He'd tested the camera angles months ago. Every job had rules. Every job had loopholes.

He already knew what the text said. He'd read it three times. He kept reading it anyway. Because rereading didn't cost anything. Because rereading was something he could control.

// What Makes This Different

01 The goal is to leave, not to win. Ace doesn't want an empire. He wants to solve $94,770 and disappear.
02 Psychological thriller elements. Panic attacks. Insomnia. Code-switching trauma. The horror of watching a good man fracture.
03 Digital age cat-and-mouse. Encrypted apps, crypto, gig economy exploitation vs. financial tracking and surveillance tech.
04 Hyper-realistic math. Actual numbers. Actual consequences. The grind isn't glamorous. Violence isn't cool.
05 A good man doing bad things. He throws up after violence. He cries alone in his car. He goes to church and feels like a hypocrite.

// Series Overview

"The game doesn't care why you're playing."

Rain City is a multi-season novel series following Ace's descent from small-time problem-solver to a player so deep in the game he can no longer see the exit — even as he keeps reaching for it.
