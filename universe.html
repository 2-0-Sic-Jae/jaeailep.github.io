The World of
Yun-Ka Teru

The Spirit-Woven Sphere — a planet shaped by ancient clan wars, elemental nations, and one sealed imbalance that was never supposed to wake up.

Chapter I

The World

Yun-Ka Teru — ancient name meaning "The Spirit-Woven Sphere." A planet that looks, from the outside, like ours. Cities. Governments. Satellites and cyber networks and industrial empires humming through steel and concrete. But beneath the surface of everything — woven into the very geography, the weather patterns, the bloodlines — is a second world that most people will never see.

"The modern world is a mask. Beneath it, the clans still move. The spirits still remember. The old war is still being fought — just in suits instead of armor."

Yun-Ka Teru exists in three simultaneous layers — all real, all present, all overlapping. Most civilians only live in the first. Spirit-Bound individuals can perceive the second. Only the Ankh'Yami Ka'Tet Wujiin can navigate all three.

I 🌐

The Physical Modern World

A technological planet of megacities, international politics, militaries, satellites, and covert agencies. Everything that looks like the world you know — but secretly controlled, nation by nation, by the clans who built it.

Visible to all · Population ~8 Billion

II 🔥

The Elemental Nations

Hidden spirit-geographic countries that overlap the modern world. Regular civilians can't see the borders — but Spirit-Bound can. Each elemental nation is shaped by ancient wars and natural resonance. Each governs one major clan.

Visible to Spirit-Bound · ~500M users

III 𓂀

The Spirit-Scarred World

The true geography beneath everything. Continent-sized rifts left by ancient wars. A sea that perfectly mirrors the sky. Floating lakes. A cave network beneath the entire world. The Spirit Scar — a colossal central crater where the First Man sealed what should never have been unsealed.

Ankh'Yami only · The Hidden Axis

The supercontinent of Vael'Koth forms the heart of the elemental nations — a massive, uneven landmass shaped like a four-pointed star, each arm dominated by one elemental nation, with the Spirit Scar at the center: a colossal ancient crater, permanently wreathed in spirit fog, where the First Man sealed the Great Imbalance. No civilian knows it exists. Every clan knows what's inside it. No one wants it to open.

Surrounding Vael'Koth are three minor subcontinents and dozens of elemental islands — each shaped by centuries of spiritual cataclysm. From above, the continent pulses: the western arm glows deep red, the south shimmers turquoise, the east is white and electric, the north is amber and brown. At the center, a black-blue cracked basin churns with storm spiral. Ancient glyph-lines — the ley-veins — connect all four arms back to the scar. Visible only faintly, only to those who know how to look.

Chapter II

The Nations

Four elemental nations govern the surface of Yun-Ka Teru — each bound to an arm of the Vael'Koth supercontinent, each built atop the ruins of ancient clan wars. They appear to the modern world as sovereign governments. They are, in truth, spiritual empires that have never stopped fighting the same war — just with different weapons.

And then there is the fifth. The one that doesn't appear on any map.

🔥

Fire · Industry · Dominance

Ka'Rai

Pyreforge Dominion — The Western Arm

CapitalCinderforge Citadel
ElementsFire · Lava · Plasma · Heat-Tech
Combat StyleIron-Pulse (Bajiquan-based)

The Ka'Rai occupy a western arm of volcanic plains, obsidian ridges, and storm-fire deserts. Their capital is a tech-magic industrial city built inside cooled lava tubes — a monument to the belief that fire and industry are the same force. They bypass the ancient Taming process using external Siphons and mechanical conduits, fusing spirit energy directly into weapons technology. The most overtly aggressive of the four nations, they have never stopped wanting to own the Spirit Scar.

The most politically dangerous of the four nations, they never take a step you can see coming.

Equilibrium · The Hidden · The Axis

Ankh'Yami Ka'Tet Wujiin

The Fifth Clan — Not a Nation. A Covenant.

LocationEverywhere. Nowhere. Recorded as extinct.
ElementsAll · Full Circuit Mastery
Exclusive AbilitySpirit Animal Bond — only clan with this power
Primary SanctuaryVeilstone Ruins — never been breached

The Ankh'Yami Ka'Tet Wujiin are not a nation. They erased themselves from every record, every map, every government database. They exist in five simultaneous location types — the Veilstone Ruins, a moving sanctuary that shifts between all four national borders each season, hidden minor bases beneath magma vents and deep-sea reefs, dimensional fold sanctuaries, and the Spirit Scar itself. They are the only clan that remained true to the original Vertical Circuit — the only ones the First Man trusted to guard the Covenant. Their duty has never changed: intervene only when the world's balance tips toward ruin. The problem is: it's tipping. And the only person who can stop it is a seventeen-year-old boy who shouldn't exist, carrying two spirits inside him where there should only ever be one.

Chapter III

The Spirit SparC

Every human born on Yun-Ka Teru carries a dormant link to the spirit realm — a seed of power descended from the First Man, whose bloodline spread across the world over millennia. This is Spirit Majic: the fundamental energy manipulation system that billions of people can access through training, trauma, or ritual.

But Spirit Majic and Spirit Animals are not the same thing. The four elemental nations have spent centuries conflating them. The Ankh'Yami have never made that mistake.

"The SparC is the engine. The spirit is the soul inside it. Confuse the two and you become exactly what the First Man warned against — power without covenant."

The key to everything is the Vertical Circuit — the three-step internal flow the First Man discovered before there were nations, before there were clans, before there was anything but a man who understood what the human body actually was.

An untamed spirit is not a companion. It is a parasite. It will attempt to reverse the flow, consuming its host's Pineal Insight until they are left as a "Hollow" — a body with a Solar Plexus but no soul. This is why the Law of Taming exists. This is why the Ankh'Yami exist. And this is why the existence of Yong' Kheru — who carries two spirits simultaneously — is considered, by every Elder in every clan, to be physically impossible.

The Vertical Circuit

01

Pineal Ignition

The "Eye of the Soul" draws the spiritual frequency inward. The first gate. Without this, nothing flows. This is the step the Mizuchi have mastered beyond any other clan.

→
02

Solar Descent

The energy descends the spine to the Solar Furnace — the body's core combustion chamber. The Doro have made this step their entire identity, hardening the furnace until their bodies become architecture.

→
03

The Taming

Subjugating the wild spirit-essence into focused Manifestation. The step every clan has shortcutted. The step the Ankh'Yami have never compromised on. The entire world's problem — in three words.

Power Tiers

I

Spirit Majic — Universal Access

~500 Million users worldwide · All clans · All nations

The baseline. Elemental manipulation — fire, water, air, earth. Enhanced physical abilities. Energy sensing and projection. Spiritual awareness. The power that billions carry dormant and ~500 million have awakened. Each of the four nations specializes in a different distortion of this: Ka'Rai weaponize it, Mizuchi perceive through it, Tian'Feng accelerate with it, Doro fortify via it.

II

Spirit Animals — Ankh'Yami Exclusive

~52,000 bearers · Ankh'Yami bloodline only (99.96%) · Rare First Man descendants (0.04%)

A sacred bond between human soul and Spirit Animal — a phenomenon the First Man gave exclusively to his primary bloodline, those who "protect balance rather than pursue power." Beyond standard Spirit Majic, a Spirit Animal bond grants: a living spiritual companion, 2.5–3x amplified elemental power, extended lifespan, Spirit Realm access, and spiritual fusion abilities. The SparC ignites through trauma, grief, or revelation — the Combustion Moment. Once ignited, the Taming must begin immediately, or the spirit consumes the host.

Ω

The Dual Spirit — Yong' Kheru Only

One person. Ever. In all of recorded history.

In the entire history of the Vertical Circuit, a single Pineal Gland has only ever resonated with a single spirit frequency. To hold two is to force two opposing wavelengths through a single needle-point. Mathematically, it results in Total Neural Collapse. Yong' Kheru is the anomaly. His Pineal Gland ignited with two distinct signatures: Sa'Fyn the Golden Falcon, and Sol'Vahr the Shadow Wolf. He is the only one who can manifest both the Animal and the Weapon simultaneously — but doing so pushes his Vertical Circuit to the edge of disintegration. He holds the Original Resonance: the ability to bridge light and shadow as the First Man once did. He is either the proof that balance is possible, or proof that it was never real.

Chapter IV

The Origin

Before the rise of nations, before governments learned to weaponize bloodlines, the world was chaos. Spirit and matter walked as one — but humans had forgotten their bond to the unseen. Then came the First Man.

No one remembers his true name. He is said to have been more spirit than flesh — a living bridge between worlds, able to commune with all living essence: the shadows of wolves, the flight of falcons, the calm of rivers, the fire buried beneath the earth. He harnessed what would later be called Spirit Majic, restoring harmony where none existed. Then he disappeared into the Veilstone Ruins after sealing away a great imbalance that nearly destroyed the earth — leaving behind one instruction and one institution: the Ankh'Yami Ka'Tet Wujiin, the Guardians of the Covenant.

"He didn't leave because the work was finished. He left because he knew it would never be finished — and someone had to watch the door."
Pre-History
The First Age

The First Flame & The Vertical Circuit

Before nations, before clans, the First Man discovers the Vertical Circuit — the realization that the human body is a lightning rod for the divine. He masters the three-step flow: Pineal Ignition, Solar Descent, the Taming. He forms the Ankh'Yami as Guardians of this knowledge. Then he seals the Great Imbalance in the Veilstone Ruins — at a cost no one fully recorded — and disappears.

The Splintering
Centuries Later

One Covenant Becomes Five Clans

Power attracts hunger. As generations passed, many sought the Communion without earning it. The original Ankh'Yami bloodline fractured into factions. Ka'Rai chose fire and weaponry, bypassing the Taming with mechanical Siphons. Mizuchi chose water and illusion, ruling through perception and shadow politics. Tian'Feng chose air and surveillance, becoming the world's unseen eyes. Doro chose earth and endurance, becoming the architects of civilization. Each clan eventually merged with a nation — their governments fusing with their powers until politics and spirituality became one and the same. Only the Ankh'Yami remained true to the original flow, erasing themselves from all records.

20 Years Ago

The Great Spirit Raid — Nahlira is Taken

The Ankh'Yami Ka'Tet Wujiin suffer a catastrophic internal betrayal. During a covert operation, Nahlira Wujiin — Kheru's mother — is captured by forces operating within the clan's own Elder structure. The Traitor Elders begin their long game. No one in the outside world knows it happened. Inside the clan, the story told is a different one.

10 Years Ago

The Eclipture Requiem — The Night of the Seal

Nahlira Wujiin sacrifices herself to seal the Abyssal Veil — a breach in the Spirit Scar that would have unmade everything. She goes into the light. Two feathers fall into a seven-year-old boy's hands. Yong' Kheru's Pineal Gland ignites in that moment — and ignites with two frequencies where there should only ever be one. The Eclipture Requiem. The event that should have been impossible. The event that started everything.

Present Day
Arc 1 Begins

The Twin Horizon Reveals Himself

Kheru is now seventeen. He has spent a decade learning to control two spirits inside him where there was never meant to be more than one. The Ankh'Yami Elder Council is fractured — some want to protect him, others want to extinguish him before a second Requiem can occur. The four nations are in a cold war over the Spirit Scar, each racing to find ancient weapons buried in the ruins of the First Age. And the Traitor Elders are finally, after twenty years, ready to move. The Hidden Axis is tilting. The Covenant is breaking. And the only person who can walk through chaos without falling doesn't know yet that that's what he is.

Chapter V

The Pack

Four people. Not chosen by prophecy. Not assigned by the clan. Pulled together by circumstance, by trust earned in the worst moments, by the fact that when everything fell apart, they were the ones who stayed. The Ankh'Yami call them the Vanguard. Kheru just calls them his.

KHERU

Protagonist · The Twin Horizon

Yong' Kheru

Ankh'Yami Ka'Tet Wujiin · "Heir of Equilibrium"

Sa'Fyn — Golden Falcon Sol'Vahr — Shadow Wolf Age 17

Vanta-black hair with golden strands. Royal blue eyes with gold-void flares. Dual golden and vanta-black aura — the constant pulse of balance. In recorded history, no one has ever bonded with two Spirit Animals. Kheru has been doing it for a decade. He has spent ten years waking up every morning in internal warfare — two opposing wavelengths through a single needle-point — and choosing, every day, not to collapse. His mother sealed the Abyssal Veil with her life when he was seven. He still doesn't know she was betrayed from inside their own clan.

ZAHRA

Support · The Blooming Sanctuary

Li'Zahra Yami Wujiin

Ankh'Yami · "The Sanctuary That Moves With Him"

Aquatic Sprite Age 18 Healer · Spiritual Guardian

The pack's center of gravity. Li'Zahra's empathic resonance is uniquely amplified near Kheru — something neither of them fully understands yet. She is not a fighter by training but by choice, when the people she loves are in danger. Her healing ability comes from a place the other clans have completely abandoned: the belief that the spirit and the body are the same thing, and that what damages one damages both. She has been quietly in love with Kheru since before she had a word for it. She confesses during recovery after their first major battle. He doesn't say it back. But he doesn't leave, either.

KAIRO

Tank · The Unbreakable Fang

Kairo

Ankh'Yami · "Gravity Tank · Sworn Brother"

The Great Bear Earth · Gravity Trust: 100/100

The only person Kheru has never once doubted. Sworn brother — not by blood, but by every version of the word that matters more. Kairo is a gravitational force — literally. His Spirit Animal grants him the ability to warp gravity fields, and his physical presence alone shifts the weight of every room he walks into. Where Kheru thinks and Li'Zahra feels, Kairo acts. He is the unmovable thing that lets the others move. He appears to die in Arc 3's finale. Ur'Zan'Ra uses the earth itself to preserve him. The moment the pack believes he is gone is one of the series' defining sequences.

NA'VEEN

Na'Veen

Ankh'Yami · "The Emotional Assassin · Sa'Ruun's Bearer"

Sa'Ruun — Cuttlefish Water · Shadow · Void Half-Sister to Lady Suiren

Deep chestnut hair. Color-shifting eyes that give away more than she intends. Na'Veen is the pack's most complicated member — not because of her abilities, but because of her lineage. Her half-sister is Lady Suiren of the Mizuchi — the series' primary political antagonist. Na'Veen chose the pack over that bloodline. She does not talk about what that cost. Her Spirit Animal, Sa'Ruun the Cuttlefish, gives her the ability to perceive and weaponize emotional states — a form of awareness that is as much burden as gift. In Arc 4's finale, Lady Suiren dies in Na'Veen's arms, corrupted. The resolve that forms in Na'Veen afterward is one the story never lets forget.
